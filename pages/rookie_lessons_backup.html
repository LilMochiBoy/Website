Rookie — Level 1: Greetings & Self-introduction (你好，我叫…）
1) Goal of this level
Students will be able to:
  • Greet people and respond politely.
  • Introduce themselves: say their name, ask and answer "How are you?", say where they are from (basic).
  • Use simple pronouns and the basic sentence order: Subject + Verb + Object (SVO).
  • Use question particle 吗 (ma) for yes/no questions and negative 不 (bù).
2) Vocabulary (with Pinyin + English) — 12 core words/phrases
  • 你好 — nǐ hǎo — Hello
  • 早上好 — zǎo shang hǎo — Good morning
  • 晚上好 — wǎn shang hǎo — Good evening
  • 再见 — zài jiàn — Goodbye
  • 我 — wǒ — I / me
  • 你 — nǐ — you
  • 他 / 她 — tā — he / she (same pronunciation)
  • 叫 — jiào — to be called / to call (used in 叫 + name)
  • 什么 — shén me — what
  • 吗 — ma — question particle (yes/no questions)
  • 不 — bù — not / no (negation)
  • 很好 — hěn hǎo — very good / fine
3) Pronunciation & tones (quick notes)
Mandarin has 4 tones + neutral. Example: mā (1) má (2) mǎ (3) mà (4).
In this level, focus on tone of 你好: nǐ (3rd tone) hǎo (3rd tone).
4) Grammar points (simple and essential)
A. Basic sentence word order: SVO
我 (subject) + 叫 (verb) + Lifen (object/name).
Example: 我叫 吴丽翡。 — Wǒ jiào Wú Lìfěi. — My name is Wu Lifei.
B. Using 叫 to introduce a name
Structure: 我叫 + Name。
Polite reply to "What's your name?": 你叫什么名字？ (we'll practice simplified forms: 你叫什么？)
C. Yes/no questions with 吗
Add 吗 (ma) to a statement to form a yes/no question.
你是学生。 → 你是学生吗？ — Are you a student?
D. Negation with 不
Place 不 before a verb or adjective to negate.
我 不 是 学生。 — I am not a student.
For feelings, use 我 不 太 好 (I'm not very good).
E. Simple adjectives and linking: 很 + adj
When describing state: 我 很 好。 — I am fine. (lit. very good)
5) Short learning dialogues & examples
Dialogue 1 — Meeting someone:
A: 你好！(Nǐ hǎo!)
B: 你好！我叫 李明。你呢？(Wǒ jiào Lǐ Míng. Nǐ ne?)
A: 我叫 安娜。(Wǒ jiào Ānnà.)
B: 很高兴认识你！(Hěn gāoxìng rènshì nǐ. — Nice to meet you!)
Dialogue 2 — Asking if someone is a student:
A: 你是学生吗？(Nǐ shì xuéshēng ma?)
B: 我是。或 我不是。(Wǒ shì. / Wǒ bú shì.)
Rookie – Level 2: Numbers & Age (几岁, 多大)
1) Goal of this level
Students will be able to:
  • Count from 0–100 in Chinese.
  • Say their age and ask others' age.
  • Use 几 (jǐ) and 多大 (duō dà) for age-related questions.
  • Introduce family ages in simple sentences.
  • Practice measure word 岁 (suì) = years old.
2) Vocabulary — 14 core words/phrases
  • 零 — líng — zero
  • 一 — yī — one
  • 二 — èr — two
  • 三 — sān — three
  • 四 — sì — four
  • 五 — wǔ — five
  • 六 — liù — six
  • 七 — qī — seven
  • 八 — bā — eight
  • 九 — jiǔ — nine
  • 十 — shí — ten
  • 岁 — suì — years old
  • 几 — jǐ — how many (for small number, often <10)
  • 多大 — duō dà — how old (general, any age)
(We will expand later to 11–99 using 十: 11 = 十一 (shí yī), 20 = 二十 (èr shí), 25 = 二十五 (èr shí wǔ).)
3) Grammar points
A. Numbers formation
11–19: 十 + number (十一, 十二, …)
Tens: number + 十 (二十 = 20, 三十 = 30, …)
Other numbers: combine (e.g., 25 = 二十五).
Zero in the middle: 105 = 一百零五 (but for this level, we focus 0–100).
B. Asking & telling age
你几岁？ — Nǐ jǐ suì? — How old are you? (for kids, usually <10).
你多大？ — Nǐ duō dà? — How old are you? (general).
Answer: 我 + number + 岁。 → 我十八岁。— I'm 18 years old.
C. Adding "呢" for short return questions
你呢？ — And you?
4) Example sentences
  • 我五岁。 (Wǒ wǔ suì.) — I am five years old.
  • 你几岁？(Nǐ jǐ suì?) — How old are you? (for child).
  • 你多大？(Nǐ duō dà?) — How old are you? (general).
  • 我十八岁。 (Wǒ shí bā suì.) — I'm 18 years old.
  • 他二十五岁。 (Tā èr shí wǔ suì.) — He is 25 years old.
5) Short dialogue
A: 你多大？
B: 我二十岁。你呢？
A: 我十九岁。
6) Study tips
  • Practice counting 0–30 aloud every day.
  • Write your age in Chinese characters.
  • Ask friends/family their age in Chinese.
  • Use flashcards for numbers.

🧩 Grammar Focus
Asking about objects
这是什么？= What is this?
那是什么？= What is that?
Answering with 是 / 不是
这是书。= This is a book.

Noun identification pattern
Structure: 这/那 + 是 + noun
Negative: 这/那 + 不是 + noun
🗣️ Example Sentences
  • 这是什么？= What is this?
  • 这是手机。= This is a cellphone.
  • 那是什么？= What is that?
  • 那是桌子。= That is a table.
  • 这不是椅子。= This is not a chair.
Practice Dialogue
A: 这是什么？(Zhè shì shénme?) = What is this?
B: 这是我的书。(Zhè shì wǒ de shū.) = This is my book.
A: 那是什么？(Nà shì shénme?) = What is that?
B: 那是电脑。(Nà shì diànnǎo.) = That is a computer.
A: 这是你的包吗？(Zhè shì nǐ de bāo ma?) = Is this your bag?
B: 不是，那不是我的包。(Bù shì, nà bù shì wǒ de bāo.) = No, that is not my bag.
